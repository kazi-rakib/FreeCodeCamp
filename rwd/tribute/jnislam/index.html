Dr. Jamal Nazrul Islam

A Bangladeshi mathematical physicist and cosmologist

Here's a time line of Dr. J N Islam's life:

  • 1939 - Born in Jhenaidah, Bengal Presidency(Now Bangladesh) on 24 February.
  • 1959 - Got his Honors in Functional Mathematics and Theoretical Physics from Cambridge University
  • 1960 - Completed his Masters. As a student of the Trinity College, he finished the Mathematical Tripos.
  • 1968 - Obtained his PhD in applied mathematics and theoretical physics from Trinity College, Cambridge in 1968.
  • 1982 - Obtained DSc from the same college.
  • 1967 - Started working in the Institute of Theoretical Astronomy (later amalgamated to Institute of Astronomy, Cambridge) from 1967 until 1971. Later he worked as a researcher in California Institute of Technology and University of Washington.
  • 1973 - During 1973â€“1974 he served as the faculty of Applied Mathematics of King's College London.
  • 1978 - Joined the faculty of City University London.
  • 1983 - Published: The Ultimate Fate of the Universe. Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, England. ISBN 978-0-521-11312-0. (Digital print version published in 2009). & Bonnor, W.B., Islam, J.N., MacCallum, M.A.H. (eds.)(1983): Classical General Relativity: Proceedings of the Conference on Classical (Non-Quantum) General Relativity, Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, England. ISBN 0-521-26747-1.
  • 1984 - Returned to Chittagong and joined the faculty of University of Chittagong.
  • 1985 - Gold award from Bangladesh Academy of Sciences (Senior group). Same year published "Rotating Fields in General Relativity, Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, England. ISBN 978-0-521-11311-3. (Digital print version published in 2009)."
  • 1992 - Publication: "Islam, J.N. (1992, 2nd edition 2001): An Introduction to Mathematical Cosmology, Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, England. ISBN 0-521-49973-9."
  • 1998 - Medal Lecture award from Accademia Nazionale delle Scienze and Bangladesh National Award for Science and Technology
  • 2006 - He was made Professor Emeritus at the University of Chittagong.
  • 2011 - Ekushey Padak (second highest civilian award in Bangladesh) and Razzak-Shamsun Lifetime Achievement Award in Physics from the University of Dhaka
  • 2013 - dies on 16 March 2013 at the age of 74.
